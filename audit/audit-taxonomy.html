Audit taxonomy · v1

What you're editing, what to call it.

When the audit flags something, name it at the right layer so the fix lands in the right place. Five layers from the industry segment down to the single moment of narrative advance — plus two attributes that ride on every beat.

Industry The market segment.

The vertical's home — Wealth, Banking, Insurance, Public Sector, Healthcare, Education, Nonprofit. Industry is structural and rarely the audit target; it's how stories get grouped on the picker.

Examples
Wealth · Banking · Insurance · Public Sector · Healthcare · Life Sciences · Education · Nonprofit
Lives in
index-unified.html · geos.html · the chooser pages
Audit applies
rarely flagged
Vertical A tenant × customer pair living in an industry.

The branded universe a story plays in. Hillside × Aster Capital is the wealth vertical; Meridian × Atlas Manufacturing is the commercial banking vertical. Identified by a vertical key like wealth, banking-deposits, insurance-life.

Identifier
vertical key — used in URLs (?vertical=wealth) and in the registry
Lives in
stories/_shared/story-shell.html → const VERTICALS = {…}
Editable
label · tenant · tenantColor · preset · category · splashOk
Audit applies
Brand Point solutions
Story One complete narrative arc inside a vertical.

A vertical typically has multiple stories — Onboarding (default), Maintenance, Fraud Fabric, Intake, Workspaces. Each is the same tenant × customer playing a different end-to-end arc. A story owns the sequence of scenes and the high-level subtitle the viewer sees.

Identifier
vertical × usecase — e.g. wealth?usecase=default
Lives in
VERTICALS[<key>].scenes (flat array for default; per-usecase variants synthesized at runtime)
Editable
subtitle · scene order · scene count
Audit applies
Story arc Workspaces
Scene One act of the 5-act spine. Plays one Docusign UI template.

Five acts in canonical order: Intake · Identity · Signing · Data · Workspace. Variants run 3–4 acts. Each scene maps to one Docusign UI template iframe (Agreement Desk, CLEAR, Signing Ceremony, Navigator, Workspace) and gets its own opening headline + lede that play in the sidebar before the beats begin.

Code path
scenes[N].tag · scenes[N].head · scenes[N].lede
Renders in
The opening lead-mode card (vignette) at scene start, then the 5 beats below it.
Editable
tag · head · lede · persona · beats[]
Audit applies
Story arc Capabilities Interface parity
Beat One micro-moment of advance within a scene. Drives autoplay.

Each beat is a single click in the demo: one head, one lede, one hotspot, one persona handoff if the actor changes. "Priya selects 'New Engagement' from Hillside's intake menu" is a beat. Five per scene typically. The viewer sees "Beat M of N" in the sidebar — that's the layer they feel. Renders as a vignette in the editorial sidebar card.

Code path
scenes[N].beats[M].head · scenes[N].beats[M].lede
Editable
head · lede · persona override
Audit applies
Capabilities Story arc
Attributes (not layers)

Every beat carries these — they're properties, not children.

Persona
Who's acting at this beat. The persona-handoff gate pauses autoplay when this changes between beats.
{ side, name, role }
Hotspot
Where the eye goes — the iframe anchor (a data-hotspot tag inside the Docusign template).
{ target, x, y, tip, sub, side }
Vignette
The rendered editorial card — Fraunces italic headline + lede in the sidebar lead-mode pop. A beat plays as a vignette.
presentation only

When the audit says X, you're editing layer Y

Glance reference for matching audit findings to the right edit surface.

Audit phrasing Layer Code path you'd edit
"Maestro / CLEAR / Iris / Navigator in narration" Beat (or Scene) scenes[N].beats[M].lede  ·  scenes[N].lede
"Scene 4 lede references a product instead of a capability" Scene scenes[3].lede
