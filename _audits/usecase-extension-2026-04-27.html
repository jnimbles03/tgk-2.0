Use Case Extension · 2026-04-27

Three demos per vertical, not one.

The canonical 5-scene story sells the agreement journey. But Maestro's most differentiated work happens at two adjacent moments — orchestrating CLEAR at the portal door and binding subsequent in-session actions to that biometric proof so auth and signing stop living in separate silos, and replacing the Agreement Desk inbox with a webform-and-package intake mechanic. This proposal extends every vertical with two new use cases, behind ?usecase=, on the same shell.

Scope FINS · HLS · PS · EDU · nonprofit · 21 verticals × 2 new use cases Architecture ?usecase= sub-flow on existing shell Status framework + 3 exemplars staged for review · 36 follow-up narrations queued

Part I · The thesisThe agreement story is the headline. The other two are the proof.

The 5-scene canonical story is the right opening hook for a vertical demo — it tells the whole arc from intake to ongoing relationship. But it spends most of its energy on the journey. Two adjacent stories show Maestro doing the kind of orchestration work that makes the platform actually distinct, and they each fit cleanly into 3 scenes.

Demo 1 — Auth-Fabric. Lifted from the original eMoney/Steve fraud-risk demo. A customer logs into the vertical's portal — and Maestro orchestrates a CLEAR identity verification at login itself, not at any subsequent transaction. That establishes a biometric proof of identity at the session level (witness event #1). Once authenticated, the customer initiates a sensitive in-portal action — Maestro serves a dynamic webform pre-filled from the IDV-bound identity, and the submission inherits the F1 biometric proof, no second IDV (witness event #2). Agreement Desk then surfaces the auth event and the transaction event linked as a continuous identity thread — one audit trail, one defensible record. The differentiation isn't "we add IDV to a transaction"; it's "auth and signing stop living in separate silos."

Demo 2 — Intake Mechanics. A focused 3-scene story showing the webform-and-Maestro pattern as the explicit alternative to Agreement Desk inbound. Sender or self-service webform → Maestro packages dynamically from the selections → envelope delivered (with optional Workspaces tail for relationship verticals). This is the explicit "how the Scene 1 archetype audit lands in motion" demo — useful in conversations where the prospect is anchored on Agreement Desk and you need to show what the alternative looks like end-to-end.

The 5-scene canonical story can stay as the headline because it's the most complete view of the platform. Demos 1 and 2 are not replacements — they're the focused shorts that make the extended story believable.

Part II · The architectureOne shell, three sub-flows, ?usecase=

The existing shell already reads ?vertical= and renders one of 21 verticals. Add a parallel ?usecase= param. Default = current 5-scene canonical story. ?usecase=auth-fabric = the 3-scene runtime IDV+webform fabric. ?usecase=intake = the 3-scene webform-and-Maestro intake. The shell stays one file; each VERTICALS entry gains a usecases sub-map.

Agreement Desk is a proxy interface for Maestro. The actual orchestration — risk-detect, IDV-invoke, identity-bind, form-serve, audit-link, package-fan-out — happens invisibly in the Maestro layer. Agreement Desk is the on-screen surface that makes that invisible work visible to the demo audience. Same template across demos, different Maestro lens: intake fan-out (canonical), chain-of-custody binding (auth-fabric), package fan-out (intake-mechanics).
