Story Arc Audit · 2026-04-27

The spine assumes inbound email. Most B2C demos don't open that way.

Of 19 stories across FINS, HLS, and PS, only three open in a way that actually matches the current Scene 1 framing. Sixteen are forced into an Agreement-Desk-as-intake mental model that doesn't reflect how the work happens. The fix isn't 19 rewrites — it's one relabel and four archetypes.

Part I · The thesisThe Scene 1 mismatch

The shell hardwires Scene 1 to docusign-agreement-desk.html and writes every story's opening as "internal user submits intake at the Agreement Desk." That metaphor is built for inbound mail-room work — paper, partner submissions, supplier docs arriving from outside. It is not the dominant pattern in B2C agreement work.

In FINS, HLS, and PS, the dominant patterns are two: an internal sender filling a webform that fires Maestro to dynamically assemble the package, or a customer self-serving the same webform from a portal/app. Agreement Desk does have a real role — it's just not the default. It's the right answer when something genuinely arrives from outside: a death notice from a funeral home, a records request from a disability carrier, a partner-submitted RFP. In those cases it earns its keep. Everywhere else, it's narrating a journey the work didn't take.

The proposal is small in surface area and large in coherence: rename Scene 1 from SENDER · Agreement Desk to INTAKE, and let Scene 1 host four archetypes instead of one. The shell template can stay; the iframe target can stay where the archetype is genuinely Agreement-Desk-shaped, and otherwise swap to a Webforms+Maestro mock or a Workspaces deal-room mock.

The spine itself isn't broken. The label and the default Scene 1 iframe are. Every other scene (CLEAR, Signing, Navigator, Workspace) holds its position cleanly across all four archetypes — only the entry point varies.

Part II · The four archetypesWhat replaces "the Sender opens the Agreement Desk"

Archetype A · Default for B2C employees

Sender Webform + Maestro

An internal employee — RM, underwriter, eligibility specialist, advisor — fills out a webform. Maestro orchestrates dynamic packaging: pick products, make selections, pre-fill from system of record, generate a tailored envelope. Scene 1 shows the webform and the Maestro fan-out, not an inbox.

Sub-variant: Maestro-triggered. No human fills the form — a system event fires it (renewal date, expiring cert, BG-check clear, claim event). Same archetype, different ignition.

S1 Webform + Maestro · S2 CLEAR · S3 Signing · S4 Navigator · S5 Workspace
Archetype B · Default for customer-initiated

Self-Service Webform + Maestro

The customer hits a public-facing or embedded webform — patient portal pre-reg, mobile banking "open new account," state benefits portal, 311 QR scan, vendor compliance recert. Maestro receives, packages, and routes back. Scene 1 shows the customer's experience of the webform; the sender side appears later (often in Workspaces) to triage and complete.

S1 Self-service Webform + Maestro · S2 CLEAR · S3 Signing · S4 Navigator · S5 Workspace
Archetype C · The genuine inbound case

Inbound Agreement Desk

Something actually arrives from outside — a death notice, an external party's records request, a partner-submitted document. The Agreement Desk inbox catches it, applies AI parsing, and routes. This is the original Agreement Desk story and it remains valid; it's just not the right scaffolding for sender-driven or self-service flows.

S1 Agreement Desk inbound · S2 CLEAR · S3 Signing · S4 Navigator · S5 Workspace
Archetype D · When the work is non-linear

Workspaces-Led

The engagement is too parallel, multi-party, or iterative for a webform-and-package opening. A commercial loan with credit-officer back-and-forth. A discovery process for a wealth household. A complex permit with multiple inspections. Scene 1 opens a Workspaces deal/relationship room; documents and signatures emerge from within it across scenes.

S1 Workspaces deal room · S2 Webforms+Maestro inside Workspace · S3 CLEAR · S4 Signing · S5 Navigator

Note the scene-product order in Archetype D differs from A/B/C: Workspaces leads, Navigator trails. The shell can still render the same five scene slots; what changes is which iframe loads in slot 1, and a small reordering of slot 5 vs. slot 4 narration.

Part IV · Top fixesThe five moves that resolve most of it

Rename Scene 1 from "Sender · Agreement Desk" to "Intake," and define the four archetypes in the shell.
One shell change unlocks the rest. Add a per-vertical intake field on each VERTICALS entry: "webform-sender" | "webform-self-service" | "agreement-desk" | "workspaces". The scene tag, scene head template, and Scene 1 iframe target all branch off it. Keeps the 5-scene spine; honors the four real openings.
Re-anchor Scene 1 copy on the 10 stories where the archetype is right but the framing forces Agreement-Desk language.
Pure copy job. No iframe changes, no spine changes. Each scene gets a new tag ("INTAKE · sender webform" or "INTAKE · self-service" or "INTAKE · Maestro-triggered"), a rewritten head, and a tightened lede that leads with the actual ignition (form submit, system event, customer tap).

Modernize the 5 stories that should be self-service but currently read as back-office.
Bigger lift — the actor changes. Scene 1 opens with the customer/applicant in their portal, not the employee opening the case. The internal employee shows up in S5 (Workspaces) for triage and follow-through, not S1.

Reorder banking ($2.5M facility) and wealth-discovery to Workspaces-led.
These two are not webform-shaped engagements. Open Scene 1 in a Workspaces deal/relationship room with all parties present. Webforms+Maestro fire inside the Workspace at the appropriate moment to generate the package. Navigator still anchors S5 for repository extraction. This is a real spine variant — the only two stories that need it.

Sharpen the 3 Maestro-triggered stories so the system event is the visible ignition.
slgov-recertification, slgov-vendor-compliance, and slgov-employee-onboarding are the cleanest examples of Maestro-as-orchestrator. The current shell head names a human "opening" the case. Lead with the trigger: "Cascade's recertification window fires for Diane Park." / "Maestro detects Apex's expiring COI." / "Marcus's background check clears — onboarding fires." This pattern alone sells Maestro better than any other single moment in the demo set.
