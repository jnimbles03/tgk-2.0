MEMORANDUM

TO: Hale, M. — Senior Claim Examiner, Verdant Life & Annuity
FROM: Ellis & Weatherby LLP (outside counsel)
RE: Beneficiary designation — Policy VL-2015-048812; Insured Okafor, Samuel (d. 03/02/2026)
DATE: April 14, 2026

I. Question Presented

Whether the 2018 designation naming Adaeze Okafor as primary beneficiary, which predates the insured's 2022 divorce, remains effective under Georgia law and the terms of the policy, or whether the designation was revoked by operation of OCGA § 53-1-5 (“revocation on divorce”).

II. Brief Answer

On the facts stated, the 2018 designation remains effective. OCGA § 53-1-5 applies to divorcing spouses. Adaeze Okafor is the insured's adult daughter, not the former spouse. The 2022 divorce decree contains no provision directing reassignment of the life policy beneficiary, and the policy file contains no subsequent change-of-beneficiary form.

III. Discussion

The policy documents and diary do not indicate any ambiguity in the identity of the beneficiary (Adaeze Okafor, DOB 04/21/2003, named with address). The apparent complication — that the insured's subsequent spouse was not added as contingent — is a matter of estate structure, not policy interpretation…

IV. Recommendation

Proceed with payment to Adaeze Okafor as sole primary beneficiary. Retain memo in claim file. Carrier should not rely on the divorce decree to modify beneficiary rights absent a court order to that effect.

Respectfully,
— D. Weatherby, Partner · Ellis & Weatherby LLP (GA bar #112884)
